GHANA

Ghanaian cuisine varies region to region, and usually consists of fish, corn, rice, plantains and fufu (starchy dish made from cassava). Meals are usually served with spicy soups/stews, indigenous spices, vegetable, and more. Note that specfic dishes vary region to region. Although, common dishes in Ghana are jollof rice, kelewele, fufu, and so much more.

Utensils & Mannerisms

Forks and knives are not typically used. Meals are mainly eaten with right hand, as usage of the left hand can be seen as disrespectful. When having a meal with multiple people, wait until the oldest person has begun eating.

Allergens & Dietary Restrictions

Due to Ghana's diverse cuisine, keep in mind allergens such as fish, nuts, and gluten are common. Also, a lot of vegan and vegetarian options are available.
