CHINA

Chinese cuisine is among the oldest (5,000+ years!!) and diverse cuisines worldwide, with lots of cuisines in China (usually categorized into 8 regional cuisines). The national dish of China is Peking Duck and staples include rice, fish, noodles, chili oil, soy sauce.

Utensils & Mannerisms

When tea is poured for you, bend your index and middle fingers against the table to mimic a person kneeling in gratitude. You may also tap your finger on the table to show gratitude, though usually used if the person pouring your tea is younger than you. Additionally, using two hands to pass items is more polite than using only one hand.

DO: Rest chopsticks on a plate/bowl rim, AKA the side of your dish.

DO NOT: Stick chopsticks into the bowl, or your food as it's seen as bad luck and death omen due to resemblance to shrine incense, etc.

Pro tip: This custom tends to bleed into other cuisines, so to be safe, don't leave your chopsticks in your food!

Allergens & Dietary Restrictions

Lactose Intolerance: Meals usually don't contain large amounts of dairy, as soy milk and tofu are common substitutes used in Chinese cuisine.

Gluten Allergy: Gluten is unfortunately a prevalent ingredient in many Chinese dishes, as soy sauce, a staple, contains gluten. However, there are plenty of other dishes that are gluten-free in Chinese cuisine!

Peanut/Tree Nut Allergy: Most Chinese dishes contain some form of nuts, most commonly peanuts and (water) chestnuts.

Shellfish allergy: Shellfish like shrimp are quite common in Chinese cuisine.

Vegan/Vegetarian: There are many vegan or vegetarian Chinese dishes, most popularly containing tofu, mushrooms, and other vegetables.
