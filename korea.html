KOREA

Korean cuisine, which is growing ever popular is largely based on rice, vegetables, seafood, and meat. Spices such as gochujang, garlic, are ginger are integral to the cuisine. Meals are typically served with many small side dishes. Fermented dishes such as kimchi are particularly popular in Korea.

Utensils & Mannerisms



Chopsticks tend to be metal/heavier than Chinese and Japanese chopsticks due to high temperatures of some Korean cuisine, the most prominent being Korean BBQ.
Other utensils such as spoons also tend to be metal, made of stainless steel.
Since a lot of Korean Cuisine leave a strong aroma, keeping cleaning wipes or a toothbrush and toothpaste handy can be very helpful afterwards, though in some locations and Korean restaurants they are provided.
When dining with someone you are close with, you can drink tea with both hands.
Tipping in Korea is not customary, and could be seen as rude.
Like in many cultures, oldest person at the table comes first! The oldest are served first, and it could be seen as rude if chopsticks are picked up before them.
DO NOT: pick up rice bowls

Allergens & Dietary Restrictions

Lactose Intolerance: Dairy is largely absent from Korean cuisine. Fermented Spicy Dishes: Korean Cuisine is very spicy and many dishes are fermented, which can be linked to increased rates of gastric cancer among Koreans.
