MEXICO

Mexican cuisine is a blend of indigenous-modern Spanish cuisine, and European influence. The base Mexican diet consists of corn, squash, chile peppers, and beans. Other common ingredients include: green tomatoes, avocados, and prickly pear cactus. Note staples such as tortillas and salsas are use widely thoughout the United States.

Utensils & Mannerisms

Within Mexican Cuisine, knives and forkes are used. The most respected or honored guest sit at the head of the table. Networking and socializing over meals is a commmon practice, so taking time to connect with others at meals is important. Tipping is common in Mexico, ranging from 10% to 20%, depending on the particular location.

Allergens & Dietary Restrictions

Common allergens include dairy, nuts, and gluten. Keep in mind that many dishes can be made vegan/vegetarian, but most traditional meals are not.
